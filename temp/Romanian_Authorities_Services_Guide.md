## 1. IDENTITY & CIVIL REGISTRATION

### Category (Romanian): Identitate și Înregistrări Civile

**Responsible Authority:**
- Ministry of Internal Affairs (Ministerul Afacerilor Interne)
- Website: https://www.mai.gov.ro/
- General Inspectorate of Police (IGPR): https://politiaromana.ro/

**Services Provided:**

#### Birth Certificates (Certificate de naștere)
- **Issuing Authority:** Local civil registry offices (Cartea de identitate și pasaport)
- **Process:** Submit application to county police headquarters
- **Documentation Required:** Birth certificate application form, proof of parentage
- **Processing Time:** 3-5 business days
- **Fees:** Minimal (7 RON as of 2025 for electronic versions)

#### National ID Cards (Cărți de identitate)
- **Issuing Authority:** County DRPCIV offices (under Ministry of Internal Affairs)
- **Electronic ID Card (CEI):** New smart card with biometric data
- **Validity:** 10 years for adults, 2-4 years for children
- **Required Documents:** Birth certificate, proof of residence, photos, payment receipt
- **Contact:** Local police headquarters or dedicated ID card issuing centers
- **Booking:** Online appointment system required

#### Passports (Pașapoarte)
- **Type:** Electronic simple passport or temporary passport
- **Issuing Authority:** Ministry of Internal Affairs / Local police headquarters
- **Validity:** 10 years (adults), 5 years (children)
- **Application Portal:** https://evisa.mae.ro/ (for visa applications)
- **Required Documents:** Birth certificate, previous passport (if existing), consent from parents/guardians

#### Marriage and Death Certificates (Certificate de căsătorie și deces)
- **Issuing Authority:** Local civil status offices (Oficiul de Stare Civila)
- **Application:** In person at local town hall or by mail
- **Processing:** 1-2 weeks
- **Documentation:** Personal ID and application form

#### Voter Registration (Înregistrare alegători)
- **Authority:** Permanent Electoral Authority (Autoritatea Electorală Permanentă - AEP)
- **Automatic Registration:** All Romanian citizens 18+ are automatically registered
- **For Voters Abroad:** Portal https://www.votstrainatate.ro/
- **Deadline:** 60 days before election (for updates)
- **Online Registration:** Available via electoral portal

**Key Links:**
- Ministry of Internal Affairs: https://www.mai.gov.ro/
- Permanent Electoral Authority: https://www.roaep.ro/
- Voter registration abroad: https://www.votstrainatate.ro/
- Criminal records online: https://hub.mai.gov.ro/

## 2. HEALTHCARE & SOCIAL SERVICES

### Category (Romanian): Sănătate și Servicii Sociale

**Responsible Authorities:**
- National Health Insurance House (Casa Națională de Asigurări de Sănătate - CNAS)
  - Website: https://cnas.ro
  - Hotline: +40 0800.800.950
  - Address: Calea Calarasilor 248, Bl. S19, Sector 3, Bucharest
- Ministry of Health (Ministerul Sănătății): https://www.ms.ro/
- General Directorates for Social Assistance (DGASPC) - at county level

**Services Provided:**

#### Public Healthcare Access (Acces la sănătate publică)
- **Coverage:** Universal public healthcare system for all residents
- **Registration:** With local family doctor (medic de familie)
- **Provider Network:** Available at https://cnas.ro/informatii-publice-lista-furnizoriilor-de-servicii-medicale-din-romania
- **Types of Care:** Primary care, specialist services, emergency care, hospitalization

#### Vaccination Programs (Programe de vaccinare)
- **Coordinator:** Ministry of Health
- **Types:** Childhood immunizations, flu vaccination, COVID-19 vaccination
- **Free Services:** All mandatory vaccinations provided at no cost through primary care
- **Schedule:** Available through family doctor or public health authorities

#### Health Insurance (CNAS) (Asigurări de sănătate - CNAS)
- **Type:** Mandatory social health insurance
- **Eligibility:** All employed, unemployed (for 90+ days), pensioners, students
- **Coverage:** Medications, medical services, hospitalization, medical equipment
- **Co-payments:** Exist for certain medications and procedures based on formulary
- **Website:** https://cnas.ro
- **Application:** Through employer or local CNAS office

#### Disability Benefits (Beneficii pentru dizabilități)
- **Authority:** General Directorates for Social Assistance (DGASPC)
- **Eligibility:** Medical assessment determining disability level (Grades 1-4)
- **Benefit Amounts (2025):**
  - Profound disability (Grade 1): 463-528 lei/month
  - Severe disability (Grade 2): 232-350 lei/month
  - Moderate disability (Grade 3): 80 lei/month
  - Mild disability (Grade 4): No direct allowance
- **Additional Services:** Free transportation, personal assistance, medical care
- **Application:** Submit to local DGASPC office
- **Documentation:** Medical evaluation, disability certificate, birth certificate

#### Welfare and Food Assistance (Asistență socială și alimentară)
- **Provider:** Local DGASPC offices
- **Types:** Income support, food assistance, social services
- **Means-Tested:** Based on family income verification
- **Application:** Visit local social services office
- **Benefits:** Minimum income guarantee (Nivelul venitului minim garantat)

**Key Links:**
- CNAS Main Website: https://cnas.ro
- CNAS Information Portal: https://cnas.ro/informatii-generale/
- Provider List: https://cnas.ro/informatii-publice-lista-furnizoriilor-de-servicii-medicale-din-romania
- Emergency Medical Service: 112

## 3. EDUCATION & LEARNING

### Category (Romanian): Educație și Învățare

**Responsible Authority:**
- Ministry of Education (Ministerul Educației)
- Website: https://www.edu.ro/
- County School Inspectorates (Inspectoratele Școlare Județene)

**Services Provided:**

#### Public Schools and Universities (Școli și universități publice)
- **System:** 12-year compulsory education (primary + secondary)
- **Structure:** Primary (5 years), Lower Secondary (4 years), Upper Secondary (4 years)
- **Free Education:** Public education is free at all levels
- **Language of Instruction:** Romanian (with minority language options)
- **University Entry:** National Baccalaureate examination required
- **Ministry Portal:** https://www.edu.ro/

#### Scholarships and Student Loans (Burse și împrumuturi pentru studenți)
- **Authority:** Ministry of Education, University Financial Offices
- **Types:** Merit-based scholarships, need-based scholarships, social scholarships
- **University Student Loans:** Government-backed educational loans
- **Application:** Through university financial aid office
- **Amount:** Varies by institution (typically 600-1500 RON/month for scholarships)

#### Adult Education Programs (Programe de educație pentru adulți)
- **"Second Chance" Program:** For adults who left school early
- **Vocational Training:** Coordinated by ANOFM
- **University Programs:** Evening and distance learning options available
- **Providers:** Public and private educational institutions
- **Funding:** Free or subsidized through various government programs

#### Online Learning Resources (Resurse de învățare online)
- **Platform:** Ministry of Education digital platform
- **Content:** Educational materials for all levels
- **Teacher Resources:** Professional development and training materials
- **Student Access:** Free access to approved online educational resources
- **Distance Learning:** Fully available post-COVID era

#### Diploma Recognition and Equivalence (Recunoaștere și echivalare diplome)
- **Authority:** Ministry of Education
- **Purpose:** Recognize foreign qualifications in Romania
- **Process:** Submit original documents (notarized copies), application form, translation
- **Timeline:** 15-30 days for standard processing
- **Automatic Recognition:** EU qualifications recognized through European framework
- **Contact:** County School Inspectorate or Ministry directly
- **Website:** https://www.edu.ro/

**Key Links:**
- Ministry of Education: https://www.edu.ro/
- Eurydice Romania Information: https://eurydice.eacea.ec.europa.eu/eurypedia/romania/overview
- School System Information: https://www.edu.ro/

## 4. EMPLOYMENT & LABOR SERVICES

### Category (Romanian): Ocuparea Forței de Muncă

**Responsible Authorities:**
- National Employment Agency (Agenția Națională pentru Ocuparea Forței de Muncă - ANOFM)
  - Website: https://www.anofm.ro/
  - Address: Central office in Bucharest with county offices nationwide
- Territorial Labor Inspectorate (Inspectia Muncii - ITM) - County level
- Ministry of Labor and Social Protection

**Services Provided:**

#### Job Search and Unemployment Benefits (Căutare loc de muncă și șomaj - ANOFM)
- **ANOFM Portal:** https://www.anofm.ro/
- **Job Database:** National job listings updated daily
- **Career Counseling:** Free services at local ANOFM office
- **Unemployment Benefits:**
  - Minimum: 75% of monthly minimum wage
  - Duration: 6-12 months depending on contribution history
  - Amount varies by contribution period (3-20+ years)
- **Eligibility:** 12+ months contributions in last 24 months, registered at ANOFM
- **Application:** Submit at local employment office with required documents
- **Guide:** https://www.anofm.ro/wp-content/uploads/2024/02/ANOFM-guide-ENG.pdf

#### Worker Protections and Labor Rights (Protecția drepturilor muncitorilor)
- **Legal Framework:** Law no. 53/2003 (Labor Code)
- **Protections:** Minimum wage, working hours limits, safe working conditions
- **Enforcement:** ITM oversight and inspections
- **Dispute Resolution:** Labor courts and mediation services
- **Employee Representation:** Works councils and trade unions

#### Workplace Safety Regulations (Regulamente de siguranță la locul de muncă - ITM)
- **Authority:** Territorial Labor Inspectorate (ITM)
- **Inspection:** Regular workplace safety inspections by ITM
- **Requirements:** Employer must maintain safe conditions and provide protective equipment
- **Accident Reporting:** Mandatory reporting within 24 hours
- **Fines:** Non-compliance results in fines (300-200,000 lei depending on violation)
- **Safety Representatives:** Required in workplaces with 50+ employees

#### Skills Training and Apprenticeships (Formare profesională și ucenicie)
- **Provider:** ANOFM in partnership with educational institutions
- **Apprenticeship Contract:** Law no. 279/2005 governs workplace apprenticeships
- **Duration:** Typically 1-2 years
- **Employer Support:** Employers receive 2,250 RON/month per apprentice
- **Certification:** Upon completion, recognized professional qualification awarded
- **Free Training:** ANOFM-funded training for unemployed persons

#### Work Permits for Foreigners (Permise de muncă pentru străini)
- **Authority:** General Inspectorate for Immigration (IGI)
- **Website:** https://igi.mai.gov.ro/
- **Annual Quota:** 100,000 permits issued per year (2025)
- **Eligibility:** Employment contract with Romanian employer
- **Process:** Employer applies through IGI
- **Duration:** 1-3 years depending on contract
- **Documents Required:** Employment contract, qualifications, medical certificate
- **Cost:** Fee varies by permit type

**Key Links:**
- ANOFM: https://www.anofm.ro/
- ANOFM Guide (English): https://www.anofm.ro/wp-content/uploads/2024/02/ANOFM-guide-ENG.pdf
- General Inspectorate for Immigration: https://igi.mai.gov.ro/
- Labor Inspectorate information through regional offices

## 5. TAXES & REVENUE

### Category (Romanian): Taxe și Impozite

**Responsible Authorities:**
- National Agency for Fiscal Administration (Agenția Națională de Administrare Fiscală - ANAF)
  - Website: https://www.anaf.ro/
  - Address: Str. Apolodor nr. 17, Sector 5, Bucharest 050741
- Ministry of Finance (Ministerul Finanțelor)
  - Website: https://www.mfinante.gov.ro/

**Services Provided:**

#### Income Tax Filing and Refunds (Declarații și restituiri impozit pe venit - ANAF)
- **Filing Deadline:** May 25 each year for previous year taxes
- **Online Platform:** https://www.anaf.ro/
- **E-Filing:** Mandatory for most taxpayers
- **Tax Rates:** Progressive from 10-40% depending on income
- **Refunds:** Processed within 30-60 days of approval
- **Support:** Free tax guidance at ANAF offices and hotline

#### Property Tax Payments (Plăți impozit pe proprietate)
- **Type:** Annual property tax on real estate
- **Rate:** 0.1-1.5% depending on municipality and property value
- **Payment:** Online through ANAF portal or in person
- **Deadline:** Varies by municipality (typically end of calendar year)
- **Assessment:** Based on cadastral value

#### Business Tax Compliance (Conformitate fiscală pentru afaceri)
- **Authority:** ANAF oversight
- **Requirements:** Companies must file annual financial reports
- **Audit Risk:** Companies selected based on risk profile
- **Documentation:** Companies must maintain records for 8 years
- **Support:** ANAF provides guidance documents and training

#### VAT and Customs Duties (TVA și taxe vamale)
- **VAT Rate:** Standard 19% on most goods and services
- **Reduced Rates:** 5% (basic food, children's products) and 9% (certain services)
- **Import Duties:** Coordinated with EU customs regulations
- **Customs Authority:** National Customs Authority under Ministry of Finance
- **Registration:** VAT registration required for businesses exceeding threshold

#### Tax Payment Plans and Deferrals (Planuri de plată și amânări taxe)
- **Availability:** Available for businesses facing financial difficulties
- **Application:** Submit to ANAF
- **Terms:** Negotiable based on circumstances
- **Conditions:** Usually requires collateral or payment schedule
- **Contact:** Local ANAF office

**Key Links:**
- ANAF: https://www.anaf.ro/
- Ministry of Finance: https://www.mfinante.gov.ro/
- Tax Filing Portal: https://www.anaf.ro/ (e-Filing section)
- Payment Options: Multiple online banking platforms supported

## 6. PUBLIC SAFETY & JUSTICE

### Category (Romanian): Siguranță Publică și Justiție

**Responsible Authorities:**
- Ministry of Internal Affairs (Ministerul Afacerilor Interne)
  - Website: https://www.mai.gov.ro/
  - General Inspectorate of Police (IGPR): https://politiaromana.ro/
- Ministry of Justice (Ministerul Justiției)
  - Website: https://www.just.ro/
- National Authority for Consumer Protection (ANPC)
  - Website: https://anpc.ro/
- High Court of Cassation and Justice (ICCJ)
  - Website: https://www.iccj.ro/

**Services Provided:**

#### Police and Emergency Services (Poliție și servicii de urgență - 112)
- **Emergency Number:** 112 (universal emergency)
- **Police Hotline:** Varies by county
- **Services:** Crime prevention, investigation, public order maintenance
- **Response Time:** 5-15 minutes depending on location
- **Reporting:** Can be done in person, by phone, or online at https://politiaromana.ro/
- **IGPR Structure:** 15 county inspectorates plus specialized units

#### Court and Legal Aid Services (Instanțe și asistență juridică)
- **Authority:** Ministry of Justice
- **Court System:** District courts, appellate courts, High Court of Cassation and Justice
- **Portal:** https://www.just.ro/ (access to cases, procedures, contact information)
- **Legal Aid:** Free or subsidized legal assistance for low-income persons
- **Procedure:** Apply through court for legal aid appointment
- **Lawyer Directory:** Available through bar association (Baroul României)

#### Crime Reporting (Raportare infracțiuni)
- **Methods:** In person at police station, by phone (112), or online
- **Online Portal:** https://politiaromana.ro/ (some crimes can be reported online)
- **Investigation:** Police conduct investigation and forward to prosecutor
- **Victim Support:** Services available for crime victims
- **Anonymous Reporting:** Some platforms allow anonymous tips

#### Consumer Protection Laws (Legi de protecție a consumatorului - ANPC)
- **Authority:** National Authority for Consumer Protection (ANPC)
- **Website:** https://anpc.ro/
- **Scope:** Protection in commercial transactions, product safety, unfair practices
- **Complaint Process:** File complaint with ANPC (regional or national office)
- **Timeline:** Resolution within 30 days of filing
- **Regional Offices:** 51 offices across Romania with trained commissioners
- **Online Services:** Complaint filing available through ANPC website

#### Criminal Record Certificates (Certificate de cazier judiciar)
- **Issuing Authority:** General Inspectorate of Police (IGPR)
- **Online Issuance:** https://certificat-cazier.mai.gov.ro/
- **Validity:** 6 months from issuance date
- **Cost:** Free
- **Processing:** Immediate for online requests
- **Uses:** Employment, travel, custody matters

**Key Links:**
- Ministry of Internal Affairs: https://www.mai.gov.ro/
- General Inspectorate of Police: https://politiaromana.ro/
- Ministry of Justice: https://www.just.ro/
- ANPC (Consumer Protection): https://anpc.ro/
- Criminal Records: https://certificat-cazier.mai.gov.ro/
- ICCJ: https://www.iccj.ro/

## 7. TRANSPORTATION & MOBILITY

### Category (Romanian): Transport și Mobilitate

**Responsible Authorities:**
- Ministry of Internal Affairs (Ministerul Afacerilor Interne)
  - Department for Road Traffic (DRPCIV)
- Romanian Border Police (Poliția de Frontieră): https://www.politiadefrontiera.ro/
- Local transport authorities for public transit

**Services Provided:**

#### Driver's Licenses (Permise de conducere - DRPCIV)
- **Issuing Authority:** County DRPCIV offices (Department for Road Traffic)
- **License Categories:** AM, A1, A2, B, BE, C, CE, D, DE (motorcycle to truck categories)
- **Age Requirements:** 16 (AM), 18 (B), 24 (D), varies by category
- **Validity:** 10 years (adults), renewable upon expiration
- **Exchange Process:** Foreign licenses can be exchanged through DRPCIV
- **Exam Requirements:** Theory test (26 questions, need 22 pass) + practical driving test
- **Documents:** ID, medical certificate, psychological evaluation, proof of driving school
- **Cost:** ~68 lei per license + driving school fees (2000-3000 lei total)
- **Appointment:** Online booking required (typically 30+ day wait)

#### Vehicle Registration (Înmatriculare vehicule)
- **Authority:** County DRPCIV offices
- **Process:** Registration certificate issued upon purchase of new vehicle
- **Documentation:** Purchase contract, proof of ownership, insurance certificate
- **Fee:** Varies by vehicle type and engine size
- **Plate Format:** EU-standard license plates with Romanian designations
- **Annual Requirements:** Vehicle inspection (ITP), insurance (CASCO/RCA)

#### Public Transit Information (Informații transport public)
- **Service:** Provided by municipal transport companies (STB in Bucharest, etc.)
- **Methods:** Buses, trams, metro systems, trolleybuses
- **Passes:** Day passes, monthly passes, student discounts available
- **Information:** Available through municipal websites or transit company offices
- **Accessibility:** Services for persons with disabilities available

#### Traffic Violations and Fines (Încălcări de trafic și amenzi)
- **Authority:** Police and traffic enforcement officers
- **Violations:** Speed violations, illegal parking, drunk driving, seat belt violations
- **Fine Amounts:** 100 RON (minor) to 5000+ RON (serious violations)
- **Payment:** Can be paid immediately at traffic stop or at post office within deadline
- **Point System:** Driving license points may be deducted for serious violations
- **Appeal:** Can be contested through administrative appeal process

#### Road Safety Programs (Programe de siguranță rutieră)
- **Authority:** Ministry of Internal Affairs
- **Initiatives:** Speed enforcement campaigns, drunk driving prevention, winter safety
- **Public Campaigns:** Annual safety awareness campaigns
- **Training:** Defensive driving courses offered through driving schools

#### Vehicle Inspection (ITP - Inspecție Tehnică Periodică)
- **Requirement:** Annual mandatory technical inspection for all vehicles
- **Fee:** ~50-80 RON per inspection
- **Duration:** Valid 12 months from date of inspection
- **Providers:** Authorized inspection stations nationwide
- **Renewal:** Must be completed before expiration
- **Renewal Timing:** Best done early in inspection period to ensure compliance

**Key Links:**
- Ministry of Internal Affairs: https://www.mai.gov.ro/
- Border Police: https://www.politiadefrontiera.ro/
- STB (Bucharest Transit): https://www.stb.ro/
- Local DRPCIV offices: Contact through county prefectures

## 8. BUSINESS & TRADE

### Category (Romanian): Afaceri și Comerț

**Responsible Authorities:**
- National Trade Register Office (Oficiul Național al Registrului Comerțului - ONRC)
  - Website: https://www.onrc.ro/
- National Agency for Fiscal Administration (ANAF): https://www.anaf.ro/
- Ministry of Economy, Entrepreneurship and Tourism

**Services Provided:**

#### Business Registration (Înregistrare firme - ONRC)
- **Authority:** ONRC
- **Portal:** https://www.onrc.ro/ (online and in-person registration)
- **Process:** 
  1. Verify unique business name availability (3 options)
  2. Prepare incorporation documents
  3. Submit application with ONRC (online or in person)
  4. Pay registration fee (varies by business type, ~50-500 RON)
  5. Receive registration certificate and CUI (tax ID)
- **Timeline:** Same-day approval for most online applications
- **Legal Entities:** SRL (Limited Liability Company), SA (Joint-stock), SNC (General Partnership)
- **Documentation:** Articles of Association, identification of shareholders, registered address proof
- **Cost:** Minimal fee (typically EUR 5-10 equivalent)

#### Trade Regulations and Permits (Reglementări și permise comerciale)
- **Authority:** ONRC and various sectoral authorities
- **Requirements:** Vary by business type (food service, construction, retail, etc.)
- **Permits:** May require additional authorization from health, labor, environmental agencies
- **Process:** Apply through relevant authorities after business registration
- **Timeline:** 10-30 days depending on permit type
- **Fees:** Vary by sector and complexity

#### Small Business Grants and Incentives (Granturi și stimulente pentru IMM-uri)
- **Provider:** Ministry of Economy and various EU programs
- **Funding:** European funds through ROPs (Regional Operational Programmes)
- **Eligibility:** Small and medium enterprises (SMEs) with 1-250 employees
- **Types:** Grants for startups, training, innovation, digitalization
- **Application:** Through ministry portal or designated intermediaries
- **Website:** https://oportunitati-ue.gov.ro/

#### E-commerce and Digital Business Support (Suport pentru comerț electronic)
- **Authority:** ONRC coordination
- **Online Sales:** Must comply with consumer protection regulations
- **Requirements:** Display business information, clear pricing, privacy policies
- **Payment Processing:** Multiple payment methods available
- **Regulation:** GDPR compliance for personal data handling

#### Import/Export Licenses (Licențe import/export)
- **Authority:** Ministry of Economy / National Customs Authority
- **Process:** Apply for specific licenses based on product category
- **Documentation:** Commercial invoice, certificate of origin, product specifications
- **Timeline:** Varies by product (14-30 days typically)
- **Tariffs:** Subject to EU tariff schedules

#### Company Financial Reporting (Raportare financiară companii)
- **Authority:** ANAF
- **Requirement:** Annual financial statements submission
- **Timeline:** Within 120 days after fiscal year end
- **Methods:** Electronic submission through ANAF portal
- **Penalties:** Late filing results in fines (500-2000 RON)
- **Auditing:** Mandatory for companies exceeding certain thresholds (€7.3M revenue)

**Key Links:**
- ONRC: https://www.onrc.ro/
- ANAF: https://www.anaf.ro/
- EU Funding Opportunities: https://oportunitati-ue.gov.ro/
- Ministry of Economy: https://www.economie.gov.ro/

## 9. HOUSING & URBAN DEVELOPMENT

### Category (Romanian): Locuințe și Dezvoltare Urbană

**Responsible Authorities:**
- National Housing Agency (Agenția Națională pentru Locuințe - ANL)
  - Website: https://www.anl.ro/
- National Agency for Cadastre and Land Registration (ANCPI)
  - Website: https://www.ancpi.ro/
- Ministry of Transport, Construction and Tourism
- Local Town Halls (for building permits)

**Services Provided:**

#### Public Housing Assistance (Asistență locuințe sociale - ANL)
- **Authority:** ANL (established 1998)
- **Programs:** 
  - Rental housing for young people (18-35 years old)
  - Social housing for vulnerable populations
  - Public-Private Partnership (PPP) models
- **Eligibility:** No other property ownership, employed for 12+ months, income between 50-100% of average wage
- **Rent:** ~1/3 of average wage (approximately EUR 120/month as of 2024)
- **Apartments Built:** Over 33,000 since program inception
- **Application:** Through ANL regional offices

#### Property Registration (Cadastru) (Înregistrare proprietăți - Cadastru)
- **Authority:** National Agency for Cadastre and Land Registration (ANCPI)
- **System:** Integrated cadastre and land book system
- **Services:** Property boundary identification, ownership registration, rights recording
- **Online Access:** Available through ANCPI portal (https://www.ancpi.ro/)
- **Process:** Submit property documents, cadastral plans, ownership proof
- **Timeline:** 15-30 days for registration
- **Documents:** Property deed, cadastral sketch, proof of ownership
- **Database:** Electronic Bulletin of the Trade Register for property transactions

#### Housing Loans (Prima Casă, Noua Casă) (Credite locuințe - Prima Casă, Noua Casă)
- **Prima Casă Program:**
  - Target: First-time home buyers (18-35 years old)
  - Maximum Credit: 60,000 EUR (old houses) or 70,000 EUR (new houses)
  - Down Payment: Minimum 5%
  - Interest Rate: Fixed with government guarantee
  - State Guarantee: 80% of loan amount
- **Noua Casă Program (replacement of Prima Casă):**
  - Maximum Credit: 66,500 EUR (houses up to 70,000 EUR) or 119,000 EUR (new buildings)
  - Down Payment: 5-15% depending on property value
  - Duration: Up to 30 years
  - Interest Rate: Base rate + fixed margin
  - Participating Banks: BRD, BCR, ING, Banca Transilvania, CEC Bank, Raiffeisen, OTP, etc.
  - Portal: https://oportunitati-ue.gov.ro/ (Noua Casa 2024 information)
- **Application:** Through participating banks
- **Required Documents:** Employment contract, pay slips, credit history, property appraisal

#### Zoning and Building Permits (Autorizații de zonare și construcție)
- **Authority:** Local Town Hall (Primăria)
- **Process:** 
  1. Obtain cadastral plan and property documentation
  2. Prepare architectural project with authorized architect
  3. Obtain environmental approval (if required)
  4. Submit building permit application to town hall
  5. Inspection and approval (typically 30-60 days)
- **Fees:** Varies by municipality and project value
- **Requirements:** Compliance with local zoning regulations and building codes
- **Final Inspection:** Required upon construction completion

#### Rental Assistance Programs (Programe de asistență chirie)
- **Provider:** ANL and local social services
- **Eligibility:** Low-income families, vulnerable populations
- **Amount:** Varies by municipality
- **Application:** Through local social services office

**Key Links:**
- ANL: https://www.anl.ro/
- ANCPI: https://www.ancpi.ro/
- Noua Casă Program: https://oportunitati-ue.gov.ro/
- Local Cadastre Offices: Available through ANCPI website

## 10. UTILITIES & ENVIRONMENT

### Category (Romanian): Utilități și Mediu

**Responsible Authorities:**
- Ministry of Environment, Water and Forests (Ministerul Mediului, Apelor și Pădurilor)
  - Website: https://www.mmediu.ro/
- National Authority for Regulation and Monitoring of Public Utilities (ANRSC)
- Transelectrica SA (electricity transmission): https://www.transelectrica.ro/
- Local water and electricity distribution companies
- National Environmental Agency

**Services Provided:**

## 12. IMMIGRATION & CITIZENSHIP

### Category (Romanian): Imigrare și Cetățenie

**Responsible Authorities:**
- Ministry of Foreign Affairs (Ministerul Afacerilor Externe - MAE)
  - Website: https://www.mae.ro/
  - eVisa Portal: https://evisa.mae.ro/
- General Inspectorate for Immigration (Inspectoratul General pentru Imigrări - IGI)
  - Website: https://igi.mai.gov.ro/
  - Parent Ministry: Ministry of Internal Affairs
- National Authority for Citizenship (Autoritatea Națională pentru Cetățenie)
  - Website: https://cetatenie.just.ro/
- Ministry of Internal Affairs (MAI)

**Services Provided:**

#### Visa Applications (Cereri de viză)
- **Authority:** Ministry of Foreign Affairs (MAE) through embassies and consulates
- **eVisa Portal:** https://evisa.mae.ro/ (24/7 access)
- **Visa Types:**
  - **Short-stay Visa:** Up to 90 days within 180-day period (Schengen)
  - **Long-stay Visa:** For residence, work, studies (more than 90 days)
  - **Transit Visa:** For passage through Romania
- **Application Process:**
  1. Create account on eVisa portal
  2. Complete application form online
  3. Upload required documents (scanned copies)
  4. Pay visa fee (varies by visa type, ~80-100 EUR)
  5. Book online appointment
  6. Visit embassy/consulate for interview and biometric data
  7. Receive decision (typically 10-15 working days)
- **Documents Required:** Valid passport, employment contract/invitation, insurance, financial proof
- **Special Entry System (EES):** Biometric data collection at border for short-stay visitors (as of 2025)
- **Contact:** Nearest Romanian embassy or consulate

#### Residence Permits (Permise de ședere)
- **Authority:** General Inspectorate for Immigration (IGI)
- **Website:** https://igi.mai.gov.ro/
- **Types:**
  - **Short-stay:** Up to 90 days (tourist/visitor)
  - **Long-stay:** For work, studies, family reunification, business
  - **Temporary residence permit:** Transit, various purposes
- **Duration:** From months to years depending on permit type
- **Application:** Through IGI territorial offices nationwide
- **Documents:** Visa, passport, proof of accommodation, employment contract (if applicable)
- **Fee:** Varies by permit type (typically 200-500 RON)

#### Citizenship Naturalization (Naturalizare cetățenie)
- **Authority:** National Authority for Citizenship
- **Website:** https://cetatenie.just.ro/
- **Eligibility:**
  - Minimum 8 years legal residence in Romania
  - Good conduct and integration
  - Demonstrable knowledge of Romanian language and culture
  - Renunciation of previous citizenship (where required)
- **Process:**
  1. Gather required documentation
  2. Submit application to National Authority for Citizenship
  3. Assessment by special commission
  4. Government approval
  5. Oath-taking ceremony
- **Timeline:** 2-4 years typical processing
- **Documents:** Birth certificate, passport, residence permits, police records, language test results

#### Asylum and Refugee Services (Servicii azil și refugiați)
- **Authority:** General Inspectorate for Immigration (IGI)
- **Process:**
  1. File asylum request at border or IGI office
  2. Initial interview and documentation
  3. Substantive asylum interview
  4. Decision (approval or rejection)
- **Rights:** Legal representation, interpretation, social assistance
- **Support:** Housing, food, healthcare during processing
- **Appeal:** Judicial review process available for rejected applications
- **Integration:** Programs for approved refugees

#### EU Citizen Registration (Înregistrare cetățeni UE)
- **Authority:** General Inspectorate for Immigration (IGI)
- **Requirement:** EU citizens residing over 90 days must register
- **Process:** Apply at IGI office with residence proof and passport
- **Certificate:** Issued confirming EU citizen right of residence
- **Documentation:** Proof of stable accommodation, employment or financial means
- **Duration:** Issued for 5 years

**Key Links:**
- Ministry of Foreign Affairs: https://www.mae.ro/
- eVisa Portal: https://evisa.mae.ro/
- Visa Information: https://www.mae.ro/en/node/2035
- IGI: https://igi.mai.gov.ro/
- National Authority for Citizenship: https://cetatenie.just.ro/
- Romanian Embassies/Consulates: Listed at https://www.mae.ro/en/romanian-missions

## 13. SOCIAL SECURITY & PENSIONS

### Category (Romanian): Securitate Socială și Pensii

**Responsible Authorities:**
- National House of Public Pensions (Casa Națională de Pensii Publice - CNPP)
  - Website: https://www.cnpp.ro/
  - Hotline: +40 800 826 727
  - Address: Str. Latină nr. 8, Sector 2, 020793 Bucharest
- Ministry of Finance (coordinating ministry)
- County state pension authorities

**Services Provided:**

#### Retirement Benefits (Beneficii de pensionare - Casa de Pensii)
- **Authority:** CNPP (Casa Națională de Pensii Publice)
- **System:** Three-pillar system
  - **Pillar I:** State pension (mandatory, pay-as-you-go)
  - **Pillar II:** Mandatory private pension funds (for employees under 35 at 2008)
  - **Pillar III:** Voluntary supplementary pension schemes
- **Eligibility:** Minimum 15 years contributions (Pillar I)
- **Age Requirements:**
  - Women: 63 years (phased increase from 59 to 63 by 2030)
  - Men: 65 years (reached 2014)
- **Early Retirement:** Possible up to 5 years early with 0.75% monthly reduction (9% per year)
- **Calculation:** Based on contribution period and average income
- **Application:** Submit to CNPP with required documentation

#### Pension Fund Management (Administrare fonduri de pensii)
- **Pillar II Funds:** Managed by private pension fund administrators
- **Contributions:** Collected centrally by CNPP, directed to chosen fund
- **Individual Accounts:** Each participant has personal account with contributions and earnings
- **Investment Options:** Fund administrators invest according to regulatory guidelines
- **Portability:** Can change selected fund once per year
- **Account Statement:** Available online through fund administrator

#### Survivor Benefits (Beneficii pentru urmași)
- **Eligibility:** Family members of deceased contributor
- **Types:** Widow/widower pension, orphan pension, family pension
- **Calculation:** Based on deceased's pension or contributions
- **Duration:** Varies by beneficiary type (widows: until remarriage or death; children: until age 26 if studying)
- **Application:** Submit to CNPP with death certificate and family documentation

#### Disability Pensions (Pensii de invaliditate)
- **Types:** Granted based on work accidents or occupational diseases
- **Categories:** 
  - Group I: Total incapacity requiring constant attendance
  - Group II: Total incapacity, no constant attendance required
  - Group III: Partial incapacity for usual work
- **Amount:** Varies by disability group and contribution history
- **Reassessment:** Periodic medical review required
- **Application:** Medical documentation, employment history, disability assessment

#### Child Benefits and Allowances (Beneficii și alocații pentru copii)
- **Child Allowance:** Universal monthly allowance for all children
  - Amount: 292 RON/month (2024)
  - Age: From birth to 18 years (26 if in pre-university education)
- **Special Allowances:** For children with disabilities or single-parent households
- **Payment:** Direct bank transfer to parent/guardian
- **Application:** Through local social services office
- **Documentation:** Birth certificate, proof of residence, school enrollment (if applicable)

**Key Links:**
- CNPP: https://www.cnpp.ro/
- CNPP Pension Information: https://www.cnpp.ro/pensii
- Hotline: +40 800 826 727
- EU Pension Information: https://your.pensioneurope.eu
- EU Social Security Portal: European Commission's e-Justice portal

## IMPORTANT NOTES FOR USERS

### Digital Services
Most Romanian government services have online portals. Romanian citizens with digital signatures can complete many services entirely online, while foreign nationals may need to visit in person or use representative services.

### Official Language
All official transactions are conducted in Romanian. Translation services are available for foreign nationals in most government offices, but documents should be notarized and officially translated.

### Business Hours
Standard government office hours are 8:00 AM - 4:00 PM, Monday through Friday. Many services require advance appointment bookings through online portals.

### Payment Methods
Most government fees can be paid online through bank transfers, online banking platforms, or in person at post offices or designated payment centers. Credit cards are increasingly accepted but not universally.

### Personal Data Protection
All Romanian government agencies comply with GDPR and personal data protection regulations. Privacy notices are available through agency websites.

### Contact Information Accuracy
This guide was compiled as of January 2026. For the most current information, always consult official government websites as telephone numbers, office addresses, and procedures may change.
